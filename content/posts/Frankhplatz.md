In 2021, at one of the endless endings of the Corona pandemic, I moved from Munich to Vienna with A and our 2-year-old son. It was a sort of homecoming for A--who grew up in the leafy 13th district of the city--and for my son, who had been born in Vienna's General Hospital. For me, however, Vienna was familiar if still unknown territory; I had visited many times before F was born, staying only for a few days at a time, seemingly always in the short, windy days of winter, during which A and I would shuffle briskly from one café to the next, huddling over hot chocolate or indifferently made cups of coffee. I never knew exactly where I was, and seemed unable to place the places I'd been in any kind of relation to each other after I had left. What remained in me was mainly an impression of gray, treeless strips of concrete curling aimlessly between apartment blocks, now with ornately decorated façades, frescoes, bearded Romans or Greeks in profile, now stripped of all decoration, painted in muted colors, but everywhere coated in a thin layer of dust from the passing cars that were crammed into the narrow streets.
